Three stories defined markets today — and every finance student should know all three. The Supreme Court ruled 5–4 to protect Federal Reserve Governor Lisa Cook's job, cementing central bank independence and sending stocks sharply higher. The Dow closed above 52,000 for the first time, partly driven by Alphabet's debut as a Dow component (up nearly 5%). And Rocket Lab announced an $8 billion acquisition of Iridium, the biggest deal in commercial space history. The through-line: institutional credibility moves markets.
