Tuesday's selloff was a reminder that concentration risk is real. The Nasdaq fell 2.2% as South Korea's Kospi crashed 10% — triggering circuit breakers — and the panic rolled straight into U.S. chip stocks. Micron dropped 13%, the SMH semiconductor ETF lost 6.5%, and the Nasdaq 100 shed roughly $1 trillion in market cap. Then Bank of America called for three consecutive 25bp Fed rate hikes in September, October, and December — one of the most hawkish calls on Wall Street. The one bright spot: FedEx reported $6.31 adjusted EPS after the bell, beating the $5.96 consensus in its first quarter as a pure-play parcel company after spinning off FedEx Freight.
