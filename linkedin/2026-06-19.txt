Today's Brief breaks down the most consequential week in markets so far this year. The US and Iran signed a 14-point MOU at Versailles, cracking open the Strait of Hormuz and sending WTI crude below $75 — erasing nearly all the war-premium gains since February. Simultaneously, new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's debut FOMC meeting revealed that 9 of 18 members now expect at least one rate hike in 2026, stripping out the easing bias and rattling bond markets. The Nasdaq still surged 1.91% as the geopolitical relief trade won Thursday's tug-of-war.
