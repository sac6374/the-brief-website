Friday's big story isn't just an IPO — it's a structural shift. SK Hynix, the South Korean chipmaker that holds ~56% of the high-bandwidth memory market, begins trading on Nasdaq today under SKHYV after raising $26.5 billion — the largest US listing by a foreign company ever, beating Alibaba's 2014 record. The offering was seven times oversubscribed. Meanwhile, Thursday's session confirmed the week's dominant theme: investors are rotating hard into memory and semiconductor names and away from the hyperscalers. Chips are no longer a supporting cast in the AI trade — they're the lead.
