Holiday Session: US Markets Dark, Global Stocks Rally on Soft Jobs Data & Iran Ceasefire Hope

US equity markets are closed today in observance of Independence Day, but the world didn't get the memo — global stocks are broadly higher, with Europe hitting fresh 52-week highs and Asian shares rebounding sharply. The catalyst: Thursday's June nonfarm payrolls came in at just 57,000, badly missing the ~113,000 consensus and cooling near-term Fed rate hike fears. Nasdaq 100 futures rebounded ~1.1% in holiday trading, while WTI crude hovers near multi-month lows as the Strait of Hormuz reopening continues to flood the market with supply. Market data is latest available and may be delayed.

Key Points
  • JOBS MISS: June nonfarm payrolls added only 57,000 jobs vs. ~113,000 expected — the weakest monthly print since the 2024 slowdown. The unemployment rate edged down to 4.2%. Markets took the miss as dovish, with the soft number easing pressure on the Fed to hike at its July meeting. Fed Chair Kevin Warsh has signaled markets should rely on incoming data rather than central bank forward guidance.
  • GLOBAL MARKETS RALLY: With Wall Street closed, the baton passed to Europe and Asia. The pan-European Stoxx 600 hit a new 52-week high, up ~0.43%, with Germany's DAX adding 0.70% and the Italian FTSE MIB up 0.65%. In Asia, South Korea's Kospi rebounded 5.8% after Thursday's brutal chip-sector selloff, with SK Hynix surging ~10.9% and Samsung Electronics up ~8.2%. Japan's Nikkei gained 1.5%. Hong Kong and Shanghai also moved higher.
  • CHIP WRECK & REBOUND: Asia's semiconductor names staged a sharp recovery Friday after a savage two-day sell-off. The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) lost 6.7% on Thursday alone. However, Bloomberg reported Samsung Electronics is in talks with AI startup Anthropic to manufacture custom chips — a headline that helped spark the Asia tech bounce. Pre-market movers include Moderna (+10%), Honeywell Aerospace (+8.7%), and Rivian (+8.4% after raising 2026 delivery guidance to 65,000–70,000 units).
  • OIL & IRAN: WTI crude is trading near $68.56/bbl — its lowest level since late February — as the Strait of Hormuz reopening continues to overwhelm markets with supply. The UAE restored exports to over 3.9 million barrels per day, and total Hormuz flows have topped 10 million barrels daily. US-Iran peace talks face a delay as Iran's former Supreme Leader Khamenei's funeral runs July 4–9, but Qatari and Pakistani mediators say talks will resume 'at the earliest possible time.' HOLIDAY NOTE: US futures markets close early today (noon CT) for Independence Day. US equities and bonds are fully closed. All markets reopen Monday, July 6.
What to Watch

→ FOMC MINUTES (July 8): The first major macro event of the holiday-shortened week. Markets will parse the Fed's internal debate on the rate path — especially whether the 57,000 June jobs print shifts the balance toward a prolonged pause. Fed Chair Warsh's 'no forward guidance' posture makes every data release and minute that much more market-moving.

→ ISM SERVICES PMI (July 6): The first hard data drop when Wall Street reopens Monday. With ISM Manufacturing already at an expansionary 53.3, a strong services number could reignite rate hike fears and reverse some of this week's bond rally. A weak print would further entrench the soft-landing narrative.

→ SPACEX NASDAQ 100 INCLUSION (July 7): SpaceX formally joins the Nasdaq 100 on Tuesday, a milestone that could drive significant passive fund flows into the stock. Shares are already up ~6% this week after entering the Russell 1000. Watch for incremental buying pressure from index-tracking ETFs at the open Monday and Tuesday.
