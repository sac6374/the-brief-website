Micron Rips, Apple Slips: Market Split as Chips Surge and Mag7 Cracks

A tale of two markets mid-session Thursday: memory chip names are soaring after Micron's blockbuster earnings obliterated expectations, while Apple and Microsoft are dragging the Nasdaq lower following price hike announcements on consumer hardware. The S&P 500 and Dow are holding modest gains, but the Nasdaq is fighting to stay in positive territory as Mag7 tech faces a sharp rotation. Meanwhile, PCE inflation came in hot, oil is tanking on US-Iran peace progress, and Bitcoin is sliding below $60,000.

Key Points
  • Micron (MU) is surging ~16% after its fiscal Q3 earnings demolished estimates — $25.11 EPS vs. $20.78 expected on $41.46B in revenue vs. $35.84B expected — with Q4 guidance of ~$50B in revenue more than quadrupling year-ago levels. Fellow chip names Sandisk, Qualcomm, Lam Research, and Applied Materials are all rallying in sympathy.
  • Apple (-5% to -6%) and Microsoft (-3.8%) are the primary Nasdaq anchors today after both companies announced consumer hardware price hikes, citing surging memory and component costs driven by AI demand. Apple raised prices on select Macs including a new MacBook Neo and M3 Ultra Mac Studio; Microsoft is hiking Xbox pricing effective August 1.
  • PCE inflation — the Fed's preferred gauge — came in at its highest rate since April 2023, adding fuel to rate-hike expectations. Markets are pricing roughly a 68% chance of a September hike under Fed Chair Kevin Warsh, who has signaled a hawkish stance since taking over. BofA now forecasts three 25 bps hikes in September, October, and December.
  • WTI crude is below $70/barrel for the first time in months, extending a fourth straight session of losses as US-Iran peace negotiations advance. Crude prices have nearly wiped out all gains made since the outbreak of the Middle East conflict, with Saudi tankers restarting Persian Gulf exports and more ships transiting the Strait of Hormuz. Alphabet is set to replace Verizon in the Dow Jones Industrial Average before the open on June 29.
What to Watch

→ PCE reaction into the close: The hot inflation print is already pressuring rate-sensitive assets. Watch whether bond yields climb further and whether that breaks the S&P 500's tenuous hold on gains heading into the final two hours.

→ Apple and Mag7 stabilization: AAPL is the single biggest weight on the Nasdaq right now. Any reversal or buy-the-dip activity in the final session could determine whether the Nasdaq closes green or red on a day that should have been a chip-driven rally.

→ Oil at the $70 floor: WTI crude is hovering right at $70/barrel — a psychologically critical level. A decisive break lower could further pressure energy sector stocks and shift the inflation narrative, but any sign of Iran deal delays could snap prices back fast.
