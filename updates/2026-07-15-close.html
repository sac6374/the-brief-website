Tech Leads on Cooling Inflation; Apple Hits Record High

The S&P 500 rose 0.38% to 7,572.40, Nasdaq gained 0.62% to 26,269.23, and the Dow added 0.29% to 52,658.64. US stocks rose on Wednesday, lifted by gains in Big Tech, a bullish outlook from chip equipment maker ASML, and a softer-than-expected wholesale inflation reading. Apple gained 4%, hitting a new all-time high.

Key Points
  • Producer Price Index posted a seasonally adjusted 0.3% decline in June, beating consensus estimates for no change; core PPI rose 0.2%, below expectations for a 0.3% increase.
  • Amazon and Alphabet moved up around 3%, while Microsoft was higher by nearly 3%; Apple gained 4%, hitting a new all-time high.
  • BlackRock shares climbed more than 7% in midday trading after earnings topped Wall Street expectations; if the stock closes at that level, it would mark BlackRock's biggest one-day increase since April 2025.
  • SpaceX fell below $135/sh, marking a new all-time low and the first time the company has traded below its IPO offering price, down about 1.8% to $133.59/sh.
What to Watch

→ SpaceX's Starship is scheduled to launch its next critical test flight no earlier than July 16 at 6:45 p.m. ET from Starbase, Texas.

→ Traders are pricing in an 84.5% probability that the Federal Reserve will leave its benchmark interest rate unchanged at 3.50-3.75% at the July meeting.

→ PayPal Holdings skyrocketed nearly 20% in premarket trading after a report that the payments company received a takeover offer valuing it at more than $53 billion.
