Chip stocks surge as markets brush off Iran tensions; Big Tech earnings ahead

The S&P 500 gained 0.6% while the Dow moved up 0.5% after stocks slipped on Monday amid rising US-Iran tensions. The Nasdaq Composite jumped 1% as semiconductor names took center stage ahead of Big Tech results this week. Tech optimism helped offset uncertainty surrounding US trade policy and the war in Iran.

Key Points
  • Chip stocks saw another resurgence on Tuesday morning, echoing a rally in Asia, after the sector attempted to stage a recovery at the start of the week.
  • Nvidia shares climbed 1% after the AI chipmaker revealed it has taken a stake in neocloud provider Nebius.
  • President Trump unveiled a new raft of 50% tariffs on an array of Canadian goods, including beer, hockey sticks, milk, and chemicals, that risk rekindling a tit-for-tat trade war between the countries.
  • Reports that mediators are pushing for a 10-day ceasefire helped ease oil prices in early Tuesday trading after Monday's rally.
What to Watch

→ Alphabet, Tesla and IBM are among the major companies scheduled to report later this week.

→ The 10-year yield rose about 4 basis points to 4.61%, recovering part of last week's 7-basis-point decline, while the five-year climbed to 4.34%.

→ Sector rotation: Healthcare stocks rose 7.4%, energy climbed 6.4%, and financials added 4.5%.
