Primoris Services Crashes 28%+ After Hours on Renewed Guidance Cut and COO Exit

Primoris Services (PRIM) plunged roughly 28–33% in after-hours trading after the infrastructure contractor issued a second FY26 guidance cut, citing additional cost overruns and delays on solar/renewables projects, and announced the departure of its COO. The news compounds an already brutal year for the stock, which had previously fallen ~47% in early May on its Q1 miss. Separately, Best Buy (BBY) slid ~3% after hours after announcing CFO Matt Bilunas will step down at the end of July — adding leadership instability atop a concurrent CEO transition already underway.

Key Points
  • Primoris Services (PRIM) fell 28–33% after hours — its second massive guidance reset in under two months — after lowering FY26 earnings guidance again on continued solar renewables cost overruns and project delays, and disclosing its COO is departing; the stock had already crashed ~47% in early May on its Q1 miss.
  • Best Buy (BBY) fell ~3% after hours after confirming CFO Matt Bilunas will step down at end of July, launching an external search for a successor — this comes on top of the company's CEO transition already announced in April, leaving BBY navigating dual C-suite gaps heading into its Q2.
  • Domino's Pizza (DPZ) slid ~2.4% after hours on news that CEO Russell Weiner will retire, with Joe Jordan named as his replacement effective October 1, 2026.
  • The broader market closed weak: the Nasdaq dropped 1.32% dragged by mega-cap tech — Alphabet fell 5% on AI talent concerns, Amazon lost 4.75%, Meta fell 2.3%, and SpaceX (SPCX) fell 16% for its third straight losing session since its June 12 IPO.
  • The 10-year Treasury yield rose 6bps to 4.51% as investors continue to digest last week's hawkish Fed signals, where the dot plot showed 9 of 18 officials penciled in at least one rate hike by end of 2026.
