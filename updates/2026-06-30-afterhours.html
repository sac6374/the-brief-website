Nike Drops Up to 8% After Hours on China Sales Slump; Constellation Brands Pops 3% on Beat

Nike (NKE) reported fiscal Q4 2026 results that beat EPS and revenue estimates — aided by a nearly $986M tariff refund — but shares fell as much as 8% in extended trading after China sales dropped 12% to $1.3B and direct-to-consumer revenue declined 7%. Constellation Brands (STZ) rose roughly 3% after hours after posting Q1 EPS of $3.43 vs. the $3.21 consensus, with beer margins holding steady at 39% despite soft depletion trends in Modelo and Corona. Separately, the Supreme Court's 5-4 ruling (issued Monday) protecting Fed Governor Lisa Cook's independence is resonating into Tuesday's close, reinforcing central bank autonomy but leaving markets watchful over the FTC and SEC's newly diminished independence.

Key Points
  • Nike Q4 EPS came in at $0.20 adjusted vs. $0.13 expected, and revenue hit $10.97B vs. $10.86B expected — but a $986M IEEPA tariff refund inflated margins by ~890 bps, masking underlying weakness.
  • NKE shares dropped as much as 8% in after-hours before recovering somewhat; stock closed the regular session at a 10-year low of ~$40.75. China sales fell 12% YoY and Converse posted a second straight quarter of 30%+ revenue declines.
  • Constellation Brands beat on both top and bottom lines in its first report under new CEO Nicholas Fink, with EPS of $3.43 vs. $3.21 expected and revenue of $2.43B vs. $2.39B; shares rose ~3% after hours.
  • The Supreme Court's 5-4 ruling preserving Fed independence (Cook decision) was issued Monday but continued to be digested by markets Tuesday — a near-term positive for monetary policy stability, though the parallel Slaughter ruling expands presidential power over SEC and CFTC.
  • Q2 2026 ends today as the best quarter for U.S. equities since 2020; Dow set a second consecutive record close at 52,319, while the Nasdaq is up 12.79% YTD.
