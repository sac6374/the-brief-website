Key Points
  • Semiconductor surge leads market recovery: The VanEck Semiconductor ETF climbed 2.5% with Micron gaining 4.5% and SanDisk popping 7.6%, driven by AI demand and SK Hynix's U.S. debut.
  • U.S.-Iran tensions escalate but markets shrug it off: Fresh airstrikes on both sides rattled markets Wednesday, but Thursday's recovery signals investor fatigue with the 'on-again, off-again' conflict.
  • Oil retreats from spike but inflation worries linger: WTI crude fell below $74/barrel Thursday despite Middle East tensions, but Treasury yields remain elevated on persistent inflation fears.
  • Breadth improving: About 60% of U.S. issues advanced Thursday, and the Russell 2000 outpaced the Dow, suggesting a broadening rally beyond mega-cap tech.
