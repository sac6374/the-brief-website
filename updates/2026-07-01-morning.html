Q3 Opens on the Back Foot: Profit-Taking, Warsh Watch, and a Battered Bitcoin

Wall Street kicks off Q3 with a mild hangover after its best quarter since 2020 — the S&P 500, Nasdaq, and Dow are all slipping in early trading as investors lock in profits and await pivotal remarks from Fed Chair Kevin Warsh at the ECB Forum in Sintra, Portugal. A softer-than-expected ADP jobs print (98K vs. 122K prior) adds a new wrinkle to the rate-hike debate, while Nike is dragging on sentiment after cautious forward guidance overshadowed an earnings beat. Key Points
  • QUARTER IN THE BOOKS — Q2 was exceptional: the S&P 500 rallied 14%+, the Nasdaq soared ~20%, and the Dow added 12%+ for its best quarter since 2022. Now Q3 opens with a cautious tone as investors weigh stretched valuations against a complex macro backdrop.
  • WARSH IN THE SPOTLIGHT — Fed Chair Kevin Warsh is speaking at the ECB Forum in Sintra, Portugal alongside global central bank peers. Markets are hunting for any signal on rate hikes — multiple FOMC members have already projected at least one hike this year, and markets are pricing in a possible move as early as September.
  • ADP MISS COMPLICATES THE PICTURE — June private payrolls came in at just 98,000, well below the 122,000 prior and below expectations, per ADP. Nearly half the gains were in education and health. All eyes now pivot to Friday's June nonfarm payrolls report — the week's main event ahead of the July 4 holiday.
  • NIKE SLIDES TO 12-YEAR LOW — NKE fell to its lowest price in nearly 12 years despite beating on Q4 revenue ($10.97B) and EPS (20¢ vs. 13¢ est.). The culprit: weak forward guidance, with management flagging a low-to-mid single-digit revenue decline ahead and saying they are 'not expecting the environment to improve meaningfully over the next six months.' China sales dropped 12% in the quarter.
  • OIL STEADIES, BITCOIN BREAKS — WTI crude is holding near $70/bbl after its worst quarterly decline since 2020 (~30% drop in Q2) as US-Iran peace talks continue in Doha. Bitcoin hit $57,950 earlier Wednesday — its lowest level in 652 days — weighed down by $4.5B in spot ETF outflows during June, the worst monthly figure since the products launched in January 2024. Crypto futures liquidations hit $395M overnight.
What to Watch

→ FED CHAIR WARSH (SINTRA): His remarks at the ECB Forum are the day's single most important catalyst. Any hint of rate-hike timing, QT pace, or inflation tolerance could move bonds and equities sharply. Watch Treasury yields and the dollar in real time.

→ ISM MANUFACTURING PMI (10 AM ET): The June reading is expected around 54.0 after 53.9 in May. A strong print would reinforce the hawkish rate narrative and add pressure to equities; a miss could offer some relief. Construction spending data lands at the same time.

→ BITCOIN $58K SUPPORT: Crypto traders are watching the $57,950–$58,115 level as a critical near-term floor. A clean break below opens the door toward $55,000 per technical analysis; a hold could attract whale accumulation interest given over 270,000 BTC added by large holders in the past two weeks.
