Micron Moment: Markets Fade Off Morning Highs as Oil Craters and Chips Stay on Edge

U.S. equities opened higher Wednesday after a brutal two-day chip-led selloff, but the bounce has faded fast — the S&P 500 and Nasdaq have slipped back into the red mid-session ahead of Micron's make-or-break earnings report after the bell. Oil is in free fall as the US-Iran peace framework accelerates, with WTI sliding toward $70 a barrel and erasing the war premium that dominated energy markets for months. The tape is twitchy, VIX is elevated, and everyone is sitting on their hands waiting for Micron to either validate or shatter the AI demand narrative tonight.

Key Points
  • Micron (MU) is the fulcrum of the entire session — shares are down ~1.6% mid-day after crashing 13% Tuesday, its worst session since June 5. Analysts expect Q3 EPS of ~$20.83 on revenue of ~$35.75B. Management's commentary on AI demand and pricing will matter as much as the headline numbers. This is arguably the most consequential single earnings print of the month.
  • WTI crude has cratered to ~$70.58/bbl, down ~3.6% on the day and roughly 40% off its wartime peak, as tankers resume Strait of Hormuz transit following US-Iran peace progress. Trump confirmed Wednesday that Iran will impose no tolls or insurance charges on commercial ships through the strait, and a new 60-day US waiver now permits global buyers to purchase Iranian crude. Trump also instructed the DOJ to investigate oil companies for alleged 'gouging' at the pump despite falling crude costs.
  • Wendy's (WEN) is the session's meme-stock surprise, surging ~22% in early trade driven by a new CFO appointment (former Potbelly executive Steven Cirulis) and a flood of retail short-squeeze enthusiasm. Sunrun (RUN) also popped ~19%, while Hertz Global (HTZ) tumbled ~22% and Cerebras Systems (CBRS) lost ~9%. On the upside, Arm Holdings rose ~3% after price target hikes from UBS and TD Cowen.
  • The macro backdrop is getting more complex by the day: BofA and Deutsche Bank both now forecast the Fed will hike rates three times in 2026 under Chair Kevin Warsh — in September, October, and December — making BofA the most aggressive rate-hike call among global brokerages. Markets are currently pricing a ~68% probability of a September hike, up sharply from 29% last week. The upcoming PCE inflation report (Thursday) will be a critical next test. Separately, S&P Global announced Alphabet will join the Dow Jones Industrial Average before trading opens next Monday.
What to Watch

→ Micron (MU) earnings after the bell tonight: analysts are looking for ~$35.75B in revenue and ~$20.83 EPS. Watch the guidance and any commentary on HBM (high-bandwidth memory) demand from hyperscalers — this will set the tone for the entire semiconductor complex tomorrow.

→ PCE inflation report Thursday morning — the Fed's preferred inflation gauge. With markets now pricing a 68% probability of a September rate hike under Chair Warsh, a hotter-than-expected print could send yields surging and pressure growth stocks into the weekend.

→ Oil and Hormuz developments: WTI is testing multi-month support near $70. Any breakdown below that level or any fresh geopolitical escalation in US-Iran talks could generate outsized volatility in energy, the dollar, and broader risk assets into the close.
