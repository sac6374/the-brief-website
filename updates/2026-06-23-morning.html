Chip Rout Hammers Nasdaq as Iran Talks Grind On and Fed Hike Bets Build

A global semiconductor selloff originating in South Korea's KOSPI — which plummeted and triggered circuit breakers — has bled into U.S. markets Tuesday, with the Nasdaq sinking roughly 1.4% and the S&P 500 off about 1%. AI capex anxiety, Alphabet's ongoing talent exodus, and hawkish Fed repricing are colliding at once, even as the US-Iran peace framework shows fragile progress and oil continues to slide on restored Hormuz flows.

Key Points
  • South Korea's KOSPI plummeted over 4% as SK Hynix and Samsung each shed more than 4%, triggering a global chip selloff that hammered the Nasdaq — down as much as 3% at session lows before bouncing back to around -1.4% by midday. Micron fell roughly $82 in premarket after rocketing $77 the prior session.
  • Alphabet (GOOGL) continued its slide — down another ~2% Tuesday after its worst day in over a year on Monday — following the defections of two senior AI researchers: Gemini co-lead Noam Shazeer to OpenAI, and DeepMind VP John Jumper to Anthropic, raising serious questions about Google's AI talent pipeline.
  • US-Iran peace talks remain unsettled: Trump confirmed the Strait of Hormuz stays open with no naval blockade, but Iran's foreign ministry contradicted VP Vance by stating there are 'no plans for IAEA inspections,' while Iran's chief negotiator said the Strait will 'never return to pre-war conditions.' A 60-day oil sanction waiver issued by the US Treasury is pressuring WTI, now near $74.
  • Markets are now pricing a ~68% probability of a Fed rate hike in September, up from 29% last week, after new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh struck a hawkish tone. Both Deutsche Bank and BofA Global Research have revised forecasts to include a September hike. The upcoming PCE inflation print (Thursday) is the week's critical data point. AMC Entertainment cratered ~24% on a $200M equity offering to retire 2027 debt.
What to Watch

→ FedEx (FDX) reports earnings after the close tonight — first major bellwether of the week and a key read on the freight/logistics economy under elevated rate and oil-price uncertainty.

→ US-Iran negotiation headlines: Iran's contradictions on IAEA inspections and Hormuz control could rapidly reprice oil and risk assets in either direction. Watch for any official joint statement from the Switzerland talks.

→ Thursday's PCE inflation report — the Fed's preferred price gauge — will either cement or shake September rate hike expectations currently priced near 68%. A hot print could send yields and the dollar sharply higher and further pressure growth stocks and crypto.
