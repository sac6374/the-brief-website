Jobs Miss Fractures the Tape: Dow Climbs While Nasdaq Sinks Into the Holiday Weekend

A weaker-than-expected June jobs report is splitting Wall Street heading into the July 4 long weekend — the Dow is pushing higher on rate-cut hope while the Nasdaq slides under renewed semiconductor pressure. The economy added just 57,000 jobs in June versus the 113,000 expected, reigniting debate over the Fed's next move. Markets close early today and are shut Friday in observance of Independence Day, making this the last full session until Monday.

Key Points
  • JOBS MISS, SPLIT REACTION: June nonfarm payrolls came in at just 57,000 — roughly half the 113,000 expected. The unemployment rate ticked to 4.2% vs. a 4.3% forecast. Bulls see a Fed policy pivot; bears see a weakening economy. The tape is reflecting both views simultaneously.
  • CHIPS KEEP BLEEDING: The Nasdaq is being dragged lower by a continued semiconductor selloff — the second consecutive session of steep chip losses. Wednesday saw Micron plunge 10.6%, AMD drop 6.9%, and Intel fall 9%. The sector's AI-driven rally is being stress-tested hard.
  • RIVIAN ROCKETS +13%: The EV maker is the standout gainer of the session, surging nearly 13% after raising its 2026 delivery guidance to 65,000–70,000 vehicles, up from a prior range of 62,000–67,000, citing stronger-than-expected Q2 demand.
  • OIL EXTENDS SLIDE ON IRAN DEAL PROGRESS: WTI crude fell to ~$67.59/barrel, its lowest since late February, as US-Iran peace talks in Qatar continue and flows through the Strait of Hormuz have surpassed 10 million barrels per day. President Trump said denuclearization talks are 'moving along well.' Cruise and travel stocks are benefiting from the oil drop.
What to Watch

→ BOND MARKET CLOSE EARLY: The US bond market closes at 2:00 PM ET today ahead of the July 4 holiday. Thin liquidity into the equity close could amplify moves — watch yields for any last-hour signals on Fed rate-hike odds.

→ SPACEX (SPCX) REBOUND: Shares were recovering after a brutal 7.9% drop Wednesday triggered by reports the FAA added SpaceX to an internal prohibited investment list. The stock's next move could reveal how much of Wednesday's sell-off was news-driven versus structural.

→ BITCOIN SENTIMENT SHIFT: BTC is bouncing above $61,000 after touching cycle lows near $58,000, boosted by Fed Chair Warsh's comments that inflation risks have softened. Watch whether the softer jobs print and easing rate-hike odds can sustain the crypto bid into the weekend.
