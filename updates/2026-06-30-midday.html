Quarter-End Rally: Tech Leads as Iran Peace Talks Lift Sentiment

Stocks are extending Monday's relief rally into the final session of Q2, with the Nasdaq leading major indexes mid-session as megacap tech continues its rebound. US-Iran peace talks resume in Doha today, keeping oil prices subdued and nerves relatively calm. It's shaping up to be the best quarter for the S&P 500 and Nasdaq in six years — a remarkable run given the Iran war backdrop.

Key Points
  • AeroVironment (AVAV) is the session's standout mover, surging 20–30%+ after a blockbuster earnings report. CEO Wahid Nawabi told CNBC that the conflicts in Ukraine and Iran have fundamentally changed the economics of modern warfare — a tailwind the company is cashing in on.
  • Concentrix (CNXC) is the day's biggest loser, tumbling ~22% after missing Q2 earnings and revenue estimates and issuing weak forward guidance for Q3 and full-year 2026. Strategy (MSTR) also fell sharply — down ~7–8% — as crypto weakness drags on the bitcoin treasury play.
  • US-Iran peace talks resumed today in Doha, Qatar. Oil is drifting lower toward $70/barrel as markets price in a diplomatic resolution. WTI is on track for a ~19% monthly decline and a roughly 24% quarterly drop — the biggest quarterly fall since 2020. The Strait of Hormuz remains the key watch point.
  • Bitcoin broke below $60,000 and is trading near $59,270 — its lowest level since 2024 and roughly 50% off its $126K all-time high. Spot Bitcoin ETFs just logged their seventh consecutive week of outflows, with June setting a record $4.06 billion in net redemptions. Crypto sentiment sits at Extreme Fear (Fear & Greed Index: ~12).
  • Today is quarter-end, which means 'window dressing' is in play — fund managers are shuffling portfolios ahead of Q2 reports. With many Magnificent Seven stocks lagging the market over the past month, that mechanical selling pressure could create choppy price action into the close. Nike (NKE) earnings drop after the bell.
  • The 10-year Treasury yield held steady around 4.37%, near a multi-week low, as lower energy prices cool inflation fears. Markets continue to price in the possibility of Fed rate hikes later this year, with September seen as the most likely start date. JOLTS job openings data landed this morning; the full June nonfarm payrolls report hits Thursday.
What to Watch

→ Nike (NKE) earnings after the bell — the Swoosh reports Q4 results this afternoon, a key read on the US consumer heading into the back half of 2026.

→ US-Iran talks in Doha: Any breakdown or progress will move oil immediately. Tehran already signaled it intends to continue overseeing Strait of Hormuz traffic even under an interim deal — watch headlines closely.

→ Quarter-end window dressing into the 4 PM close: Thin holiday-week volume plus mechanical fund rebalancing could amplify moves in either direction. Thursday brings June nonfarm payrolls — the most important data point of the week for Fed watchers.
