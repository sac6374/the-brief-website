Oil Settles Down >4%, OpenAI IPO Delay Rattles Tech, and Iran Strikes Vessel in Strait of Hormuz — A Week to Remember Ends on a Sour Note

Friday's session closed a turbulent week as three converging catalysts weighed on markets: Brent crude settled down 4.34% and WTI fell 3.74% as tanker traffic through the Strait of Hormuz rebounded, even after Iran's IRGC struck a Singapore-flagged cargo vessel near the Omani coast — a confirmed ceasefire violation that President Trump called 'a foolish violation' on Truth Social. Separately, a New York Times report that OpenAI is leaning toward delaying its IPO until 2027 — after CEO Sam Altman refused advisers' suggestion to accept a sub-$1 trillion valuation — hammered tech sentiment globally, sending SoftBank shares down 12%+ in Tokyo and dragging chip stocks lower in the U.S. The Nasdaq ended the week down roughly 5.2%, snapping a two-week win streak, while the S&P 500 closed the week nearly 2% lower.

Key Points
  • Iran's IRGC struck a Singapore-flagged cargo vessel in the Strait of Hormuz on Thursday, pausing the IMO's evacuation of stranded ships and prompting President Trump to call it a 'foolish violation' of the US-Iran ceasefire — yet oil ultimately fell sharply on the day as tanker volumes surged to their highest pace since the war began, with Brent settling below $72.
  • The New York Times reported that OpenAI is leaning toward delaying its IPO until 2027, after CEO Sam Altman rejected any valuation below $1 trillion; SpaceX's post-IPO share price collapse from ~$218 to ~$149 in two weeks spooked advisers. SoftBank, holding a ~$65B stake in OpenAI, plunged 12%+ in Tokyo — its worst day since August 2024 — and the news rippled through U.S. chip stocks including AMD (-2.6%), ARM (-4%), and MRVL (-3.4%) on Friday.
  • The Nasdaq Composite fell ~5.2% on the week — its worst weekly performance in recent memory — combining the OpenAI IPO delay shock, Apple and Microsoft consumer hardware price hikes driven by the memory shortage, and a hot May PCE reading that keeps the door open for a Fed rate hike later this year.
  • Watch Monday's open closely: if SoftBank's rout deepens in Asian trade over the weekend and futures gap lower, the AI-infrastructure trade could face another leg down. Iraq's threat to leave OPEC unless it receives a higher production quota is also worth watching for energy volatility next week.
