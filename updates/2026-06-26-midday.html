Key Points
  • OpenAI is leaning toward delaying its IPO until 2027, per a NYT report, with CEO Sam Altman reportedly rejecting any valuation below $1 trillion. The news rattled chip stocks and hyperscalers, with JPMorgan traders flagging concerns about the sustainability of AI infrastructure spending without capital markets funding.
  • Russell Reconstitution Day is driving extraordinary close-of-day volume. SpaceX (SPCX) enters the Russell 1000 after today's bell under a new fast-track rule, forcing an estimated $22–$27 billion in mechanical passive buying. SPCX is up roughly 1.5% mid-session heading into the critical closing print.
  • WTI crude is down over 4% on the session — on pace for a third straight weekly loss — as Strait of Hormuz tanker traffic hits its fastest pace since the US-Iran conflict began. The geopolitical risk premium that drove oil above $117 is rapidly evaporating, easing inflation concerns even as Fed Chair Kevin Warsh keeps a hawkish tone.
  • Bitcoin is trading near $59,335, down over 3% on the day, briefly touching a 21-month low earlier in the session. BTC is off roughly 19% since June 1 as hot PCE inflation data, a tech sector selloff, and ETF outflows weigh heavily on risk assets. The Crypto Fear & Greed Index sits at 13 — Extreme Fear.
What to Watch

→ The Russell reconstitution close: All passive rebalancing — including the SpaceX forced buy — executes at today's closing bell. Expect a massive volume spike and elevated volatility in the final 15 minutes of trading. Russell 1000 ETFs (IWB, IWF) open Monday with SPCX exposure; Nasdaq 100 inclusion follows on July 6.

→ Fed rate hike odds and the 10-year yield: Minneapolis Fed President Neel Kashkari said Friday he now expects one rate hike this year. Markets are pricing approximately 68% odds of a September hike — up from 29% just one week ago. Watch the 10-year hold around the 4.39% level heading into next week's jobs data.

→ Sector rotation into the close: Advancing shares are outnumbering declining ones despite Nasdaq weakness — Industrials, Healthcare, and Materials are leading the tape. Quarter-end rebalancing flows may amplify moves into the final hour. The S&P 500 is on track for a weekly loss of over 1%; the Nasdaq is heading for a 4% weekly drop.
