Half-Time Whistle: Stocks Limp Into Q2 Close as Doha Talks Begin and Jobs Report Looms

Today is the last trading day of Q2 — and it hasn't been a pretty quarter for tech. US futures are pointing to a muted open as Wall Street wraps up a month where the S&P 500 shed ~3% and the Nasdaq lost over 6%, even after Monday's relief rally. The dominant macro story remains the US-Iran conflict: peace talks resume in Doha today, crude is pulling back toward $70, but Iran's foreign ministry has accused the US of ceasefire violations — keeping geopolitical risk firmly on the table. Key Points
  • US-Iran Doha talks begin today but Iran's foreign ministry is refusing direct meetings with the US delegation — led by Kushner and Witkoff — accusing Washington of violating the June 17 ceasefire. The fragility of the truce continues to overhang energy markets and risk sentiment globally.
  • Oil is having its worst quarter since 2020: WTI is on track for a ~24% decline in Q2 and ~19% drop in June alone, as Strait of Hormuz shipping has gradually reopened and Iran sanction waivers added extra supply. Brent is near $73/bbl. Today's Doha outcome will be critical for crude's direction.
  • SCOTUS delivered two market-relevant rulings Monday: the court ruled Trump can fire FTC and other independent agency commissioners, but a 5-4 decision blocked Trump from removing Fed Governor Lisa Cook — at least for now. Markets are watching for any implications for Fed independence.
  • Yesterday's session brought big headline moves: the Dow hit a first-ever close above 52,000, powered by Alphabet's debut as a Dow member (+~5%), a Comcast spin-off announcement (+4.4%), and Rocket Lab's $8B Iridium acquisition deal (+15.9%). Tech megacaps staged a broad relief rally — but the month-long damage to the Nasdaq (-6%+) is still the scoreboard.
  • Medicare begins covering obesity drugs (GLP-1s) starting tomorrow — a massive potential catalyst for Novo Nordisk and Eli Lilly as a huge new patient population enters the market. Watch both names today.
  • Bitcoin is back below $60K (~$58.5K), pressured by $6.4B in US spot ETF outflows over the past 30 days — the largest monthly outflow since the ETFs launched in 2024. Institutional rotation into AI stocks and the SpaceX IPO narrative is cited as a key driver.
What to Watch

→ Doha peace talks (US-Iran): Iran has said it won't meet directly with the US delegation. Any breakdown — or breakthrough — will move oil prices fast and ripple into equities and bonds.

→ Thursday's June Nonfarm Payrolls report (released early due to the July 4 holiday week): The bond market closes Friday. May NFP came in at 172,000; expectations are for a softer June. With the Fed pricing in rate hikes (first move ~62% probability in September), this number matters enormously.

→ Nike earnings after the close today: First major consumer read of the earnings season. Watch for commentary on tariff impacts, consumer health, and China sales — relevant well beyond just the footwear sector.

→ JOLTS Job Openings data due shortly after today's open: April printed a robust 7.6M. A hot May reading would reinforce the hawkish Fed narrative and pressure rates higher.

→ Quarter-end rebalancing flows: Today is the last day of Q2. Large pension and sovereign wealth funds may be selling equities and buying bonds to rebalance — expect potentially choppy, volume-driven price action into the close.
