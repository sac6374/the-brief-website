Markets Dark for Independence Day — Here's What You Missed

US equity markets are closed today, Friday, July 3, in observance of Independence Day — NYSE and Nasdaq are both dark as July 4 falls on a Saturday this year, with the holiday observed a day early. Thursday's final session before the long weekend told a split story: the Dow closed at a fresh all-time high while the Nasdaq sold off sharply on chip-sector weakness. Markets reopen Monday, July 6, with a packed macro and sentiment agenda waiting.

Key Points
  • NYSE and Nasdaq are fully closed today — Independence Day observed. No live US equity trading, no ETF sessions, no bond market. The US bond market also shut, per SIFMA's recommendation. Everything resumes Monday, July 6 at 9:30 AM ET.
  • Thursday's session was a tale of two markets: the Dow surged ~595 points to a new record high, led by Apple (+4.8%), McDonald's (+4.1%), and Walmart (+~3%), while the Nasdaq slid 0.8% as chipmakers faced a second straight day of heavy selling — Micron -7%, Applied Materials -7.4%, AMD -4.3%, and Tesla -7.5%.
  • June jobs report landed well below expectations: the US added only 57,000 jobs vs. a consensus of ~115,000, with prior months revised lower. The unemployment rate dipped to 4.2%, partly due to a drop in labor force participation. The soft print pushed back bets on a Fed rate hike in September — odds fell from ~64% to ~50% in one session.
  • Geopolitical backdrop remains active: US-Iran nuclear talks are progressing via Qatari and Pakistani mediators in Doha, with President Trump saying 'denuclearization of Iran is moving along well.' Progress on talks has helped push oil to multi-month lows (~$68/bbl), easing inflation pressure. Note: Iran's Supreme Leader Khamenei's state funeral runs July 4–9, likely delaying the next round of formal negotiations.
What to Watch

→ Monday open volatility — three days of accumulated headlines hit the tape at once. AI and semiconductor stocks (Nvidia, AMD, Micron, AMAT) are the key read on whether Thursday's tech selloff was a rotation or the start of a deeper de-rating of the AI trade.

→ Fed policy trajectory: with September hike odds now near 50/50 and Fed Chair Kevin Warsh signaling no urgency to hike, next week's Fed communications and any CPI-adjacent data will be closely scrutinized. Inflation still running at 4.2% YoY (May CPI) keeps the Fed in a delicate spot.

→ US-Iran peace talks resumption post-Khamenei funeral (funeral runs through July 9) — any breakthrough could send oil materially lower and further ease inflation fears, a potential tailwind for both bonds and rate-sensitive equities.
